How DFS lineups work

Build an 8-player lineup for the next slate with a $60,000 salary cap. Salaries weight offensive rating (~80%), opponent runs allowed (~12%), and the opposing primary pitcher (~8%). Player rating leans on career stats when available (about 85% career / 15% 2026 when both exist). Offensive rating is a composition of statistical rates (e.g. damage and run-production measures): each piece is compared to league baselines, then combined with fixed formula weights—not a scout’s eye or anyone’s opinion. Nothing subjective sets player rankings or DFS prices: no votes, manual tiers, or discretionary ordering—only the underlying stats and the math applied to them. The bottom 20% of the slate by salary are all priced at $5,000. Everyone else is priced on a smooth ladder from $5,100 up to $12,000 by offensive rating (no big jumps after the minimum tier). You can browse everything without an account. To compete on the leaderboard, pick any display name (up to 24 characters) and save your lineup for Sunday week slates (W#) or Wednesday slates (D…). One lineup per device per slate. When you save to the database, each player’s salary is locked at that moment — the leaderboard always shows what you paid, not today’s prices. You can edit and re-save before the deadline; if you change players, new salaries apply on the next save. Only past slates (view only) and the current slate appear below — future weeks stay hidden until it’s time to set lineups. Sunday slates lock at 11:59 PM Eastern the night before games. Wednesday slates lock at 8:00 PM Eastern on game day.
