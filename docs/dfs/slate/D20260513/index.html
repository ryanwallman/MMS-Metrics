How DFS lineups work

Build an 8-player lineup for the next slate with a $60,000 salary cap. Salaries weight offensive rating (~80%), opponent runs allowed (~12%), and the opposing primary pitcher (~8%). Player rating leans on career stats when available (about 85% career / 15% 2026 when both exist). Offensive rating is a composition of statistical rates (e.g. damage and run-production measures): each piece is compared to league baselines, then combined with fixed formula weights—not a scout’s eye or anyone’s opinion. Nothing subjective sets player rankings or DFS prices: no votes, manual tiers, or discretionary ordering—only the underlying stats and the math applied to them. The bottom 25% of the slate by salary are all priced at $5,000, so lineups aren’t built around one leftover minimum-price pick; within that group, names are listed alphabetically. You can browse everything without an account.
